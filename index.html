Project Overview

YoLink deployed IoT solutions across more than 100 service centers and warehouses for Unifirst Corporation, enabling environmental monitoring and safety assurance

Customer Challenges

As a leading provider of uniforms and industrial laundry services in North America, Unifirst faced challenges arising from operational complexity and physical scale:

  • Environmental monitoring required across 100+ service centers and warehouses, with traditional sensors proving difficult to deploy
  • Compliance requirements with OSHA and Cal/OSHA temperature and humidity monitoring standards
  • High costs and complexity associated with wiring and strong Wi-Fi coverage required for traditional sensors
  • Need for real-time monitoring and alert systems to prevent floods, equipment damage, and unauthorized access

Deployment Case Study

Real-world deployment scenarios and use cases of the YoLink sensor system across Unifirst Corporation's facilities

Temperature & Humidity Monitoring Solution

To meet OSHA and Cal/OSHA heat stress monitoring requirements, YoLink deployed a temperature and humidity sensor network across Unifirst's warehouses and work areas:

  • 1

    Three-Stage Alert System

    Three alert levels (82°F warning, 87°F moderate, 103°F high) corresponding to different preventive measures

  • 2

    Temperature Mapping Plan

    Sensor locations determined through pre-installation temperature mapping plan to cover all work areas and hotspots

  • 3

    Flexible Deployment Strategy

    Different sensor deployment strategies (single-point, dual-point, or tri-point arrangement) based on work area size

Temperature Monitoring Data Example (24 Hours)

Sensor Installation Locations

  • Packaging workstation areas
  • Work dock areas
  • Warehouse goods exit areas
  • Heat tunnel and mezzanine work areas
  • Maintenance areas and battery charging zones

Alert Settings

  • Warning Level (82°F): Send notification
  • Moderate Level (87°F): Send notification and initiate cooling measures
  • High Level (103°F): Send emergency notification and activate emergency response plan
  • Alerts triggered only during working hours (Monday-Friday, 2:00 AM - 8:00 PM)

Deployment Strategy

  • Small areas: Central single-point deployment
  • Medium areas: Dual-point deployment at 1/3 and 2/3 positions
  • Large areas: Uniformly distributed tri-point deployment
  • Sensor positions adjustable based on monitoring data

Implementation Guide

Detailed implementation steps and best practices for YoLink's heat stress environmental monitoring solution

1 Project Planning Phase

  • Review company's heat illness prevention plan to ensure environmental temperature and humidity monitoring is included
  • Designate personnel responsible for temperature monitoring project (TMO) and assign alert recipients in Raedius application
  • Develop temperature mapping plan (TMP) based on historical data and work area layouts
  • Identify hotspot areas and critical work areas requiring monitoring
