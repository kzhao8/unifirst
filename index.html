Fire Incident Cases

Past Fire Incidents and Lessons Learned

North Charleston Dryer Fire

In August 2020, a dryer fire occurred at a UniFirst uniform service facility in North Charleston, South Carolina. Employees were evacuated promptly, and the fire department responded quickly, containing the fire to the equipment. There were no injuries.

Cause

Blocked dryer vents led to overheating and ignition.

Lessons Learned

  • Importance of regular dryer vent cleaning
  • Value of employee fire emergency training and drills
  • Necessity of early fire detection systems

Fayetteville Facility Fire

In March 2024, a structural fire occurred at a UniFirst facility near Fayetteville, North Carolina. Multiple fire departments responded. The cause is preliminarily believed to be an electrical malfunction.

Cause

Preliminary investigation suggests an electrical malfunction, but the exact cause is still under investigation.

Lessons Learned

  • Importance of regular electrical system inspections and maintenance
  • Need for monitoring during unoccupied hours
  • Necessity of rapid response and emergency evacuation plans

Fire Risk Analysis in Industrial Laundry Facilities

Electrical Failures

Electrical system malfunctions, including short circuits, overloads, and poor connections, are a leading cause of fires in industrial facilities.

Equipment Overheating

High-temperature equipment such as dryers and ironing machines can cause fires if temperature controls malfunction or ventilation is inadequate.

Flammable Materials

Accumulated lint, oil-contaminated textiles, and other flammable materials can become fire hazards if not properly managed.

Applicable Regulations & Standards

Fire Safety Regulations and Standards for Industrial Laundry Facilities

OSHA Regulations

Fire Prevention Plan (29 CFR 1910.39)

Requires employers to develop a written fire prevention plan, including procedures for controlling accumulations of flammable waste and maintaining heat-producing equipment.

Fire Extinguisher Training (29 CFR 1910.157)

Requires employers to provide training to employees on the use of fire extinguishers to ensure they can take effective action in the early stages of a fire.

Flammable Liquids Management (29 CFR 1910.106)

Specifies requirements for the storage, handling, and use of flammable liquids, including the use of approved containers and safe operating procedures.

Emergency Exit Requirements (29 CFR 1910.36-.39)

Ensures emergency exits are unobstructed, clearly marked, and regularly inspected and maintained.

NFPA Standards

NFPA 1 (Fire Code)

Requires industrial facilities to install automatic sprinkler systems and fire alarm systems to ensure fire safety.

NFPA 654 (Standard for the Prevention of Fire and Dust Explosions from the Manufacturing, Processing, and Handling of Combustible Particulate Solids)

Requires hazard analysis and control of combustible dust, including regular cleaning and maintenance of ventilation systems.

NFPA 91 (Standard for Exhaust Systems for Air Conveying of Vapors, Gases, Mists, and Noncombustible Particulate Solids)

Provides guidelines for the design and maintenance of ventilation systems used to remove flammable lint and air contaminants in industrial processes.

NFPA 70 (National Electrical Code)

Ensures electrical systems meet safety standards, reducing the risk of fires caused by electrical malfunctions.
